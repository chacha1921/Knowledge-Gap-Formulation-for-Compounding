## The Question

In Week 11, I implemented cross-family judge-generator separation to prevent preference leakage — my generation model and judge model were always from different families. But self-preference is a separate and distinct bias: a judge systematically favors outputs that resemble its own stylistic patterns, regardless of actual quality. In my pipeline, the same eval-tier judge model that spot-checked dataset tasks during filtering also scored the sealed held-out partition — and models from that same family contributed to my multi-LLM synthesis tasks. My question is: does cross-family rotation actually break the self-preference path — or could tasks generated by models stylistically similar to my judge have scored higher under evaluation in a way that silently inflated my Delta A result without being detectable by my existing leakage guard?
